Introduction

Through Earth's geologic time, the atmosphere went through many changes. The Great Oxidation Event is one of the most significant events in Earth History. This geologic event occured about 2.35 billion years ago when Earth went from having no oxygen to having a small amount of oxygen.

Earth History

Archean Timeline

Earth's early atmosphere mainly consisted of water vapor, carbon dioxide, nitrogen, carbon monoxide, hydrogen sulfied, and hydrogen chloride. The Earth's atmosphere became oxygenated by the Proterozoic, which began 2.5 billion years ago. Multicellular life appear in the fossil record 1.8 billion years ago following the influx in oxygen levels.

Geologic Time Scale

The Archean Eon which began 4.6 billion years ago, is the oldest unit of time on the geological time scale.

Geological Evidence

Cyanobacteria

Cyanobacteria produced Earth's early oxygen and enabled the diversification of multicellular life forms.

Banded Iron Formation

Banded iron formations are thin bedded sedimentary rocks that first appeared in the Precambrian period dating back to nearly 3 billion years ago. Banded iron formations are found all over Earth which is evidence that the oxygen levels were fluctuating Earth's atmosphere.
