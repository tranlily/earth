Introduction

Through Earth's geologic time, the atmosphere went through many changes. The Great Oxidation Event is one of the most significant events in Earth History. This geologic event occured about 2.35 billion years ago when Earth went from having no oxygen to having a small amount of oxygen.

Earth History

Archean Timeline

Earth's early atmosphere mainly consisted of water vapor, carbon dioxide, nitrogen, carbon monoxide, hydrogen sulfied, and hydrogen chloride. The Earth's atmosphere became oxygenated by the Proterozoic, which began 2.5 billion years ago. Multicellular life appear in the fossil record 1.8 billion years ago following the influx in oxygen levels.

O2 Levels in the Atmosphere

O2 build up in the Earth's atmosphere has been described to be split into four stages.

Stage 1: 3.85–2.45 BYA Absence of O2 in the atmosphere and the oceans
Stage 2: 2.45–1.85 BYA O2 began being produced but was mainly absorbed into the ocean
Stage 3: 1.85–0.85 BYA O2 began releasing from the ocean but was mainly absorbed into land
Stage 4: 0.85 BYA–Present O2 filled the atmosphere

Geologic Time Scale

The Archean Eon which began 4.6 billion years ago, is the oldest unit of time on the geological time scale. Life began to evolve from oceans, plate tectonic activity, continents, and proliferation of prokaryote bacteria. During this time, early bacteria and stromatolites formed.

Geological Evidence

Cyanobacteria

Cyanobacteria is blue-green algae from the ocean that produces free oxygen as a waste product into the ocean via photosynthesis. This oxygen build up in the oceans eventually filled the atmosphere which marked the beginning of the Great Oxygenation Event. Cyanobacteria enabled the diversification of multicellular life forms. However, cyanobacteria is an anaerobic organism which means it flourishes from the absence of oxygen. As oxygen levels increased, the amount of anaerobic organisms decreased and caused an event known as the "oxygen crisis". O2 levels were fluctuating during this time, but eventually life evolved and Earth began to hold O2 in the atmosphere.

Banded Iron Formation

Banded iron formations are thin bedded sedimentary rocks that first appeared in the Precambrian period dating back to nearly 3 billion years ago. As photosynthetic organisms produced oxygen, there was a reaction with iron dissolved in seawater and formed iron oxide minerals which are known as banded iron formations. This rock formation consists of dark layers of unoxidized iron and bright red layers of oxidized iron. Banded iron formations are found all over Earth which is evidence that the oxygen levels were fluctuating Earth's atmosphere.
